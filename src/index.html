Reclaiming the social graph for the common good is one of the greatest opportunities of our time.

The complex lattice of all our relationships online—known as the social graph—holds immense power. Social graphs are embedded at the core of every social network, and today, most of how we connect, learn, transact, contribute and consume occurs through them. Yet social networks each have their own proprietary social graph, and they are owned by a handful of private companies who’ve become the de facto gatekeepers of our online interactions. In this balkanized system, corporations broker our relationships, exploit our personal data for their own gain, and control what information we see.

We hope you will be inspired to join us in rewriting the rules—and in taking back this essential part of the Internet for all.

We believe a unified, decentralized, universally accessible social graph is essential to creating a better Internet, one that serves us all. This new, shared social graph will give people agency over their data, control over their social networks, and helps them derive direct economic value from their contribution. With people, not corporations, in control, we can reclaim the social graph to serve public good over shareholder profit—and open opportunity for positive social impact at scale.

We have begun this work, but it is too ambitious for any one group to succeed alone. It requires the collaborative efforts of many bold and visionary communities, with skills from many disciplines. We hope you are inspired to join us in rewriting the rules—and in taking back this essential part of the internet for all.
